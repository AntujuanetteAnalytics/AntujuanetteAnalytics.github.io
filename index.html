    Location Performance: Dashboard and Written Presentation

    I was given the task to pinpoint the underperforming locations. Kansas and Hawaii have the lowest sales/revenue. One reason for this is the number of customers each location has obtained. Although the amount of sells differ by a few purchases, revenue for Hawaii is noticeably higher than Kansas. This stands out as abnormal in a data set that has a consistent pattern. After shifting through the noise, seasonal values appear to have a similar abnormal trend. Spring has the highest sells; however, Fall has more revenue.

    By calculating the average, it shows that the price for items in Fall are just a hair higher than the average item price in Spring. Prior to validating my observations, I made a brisk data driven hypothesis. My forecast is that Hawaii's demanding season is Fall and after cross referencing, this was confirmed. Hawaii's profitable season is in sync with the profitable time of year for the company which fills the sell/revenue gap.

    With these insights, a data-based suggestion would be to spotlight footwear in Kansas. It's the second highest priced category and for Kansas it's a decent consumer attraction. The key is to target market the footwear during Spring. Yes, Fall is highest in revenue however, Spring already has momentum in the Kansas market. In this scenario, jumping into an already flowing stream is more efficient than redirecting it.
